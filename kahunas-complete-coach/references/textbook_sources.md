### 2. Fatigue-Recover-Adapt-1.pdf
**Content:** Fatigue management and recovery optimization
**Key Concepts:**
- **Fitness-Fatigue Model:** Performance = Fitness - Fatigue
- **Fatigue Types:**
  - Acute: ATP depletion, metabolite accumulation, CNS disruption
  - Chronic: Glycogen depletion, hormonal disruption, tissue trauma
  - Peripheral: Localized to working muscles
  - Systemic: Cumulative whole-body fatigue
  
- **Signs of High Fatigue:**
  - Increased RPE/stress
  - Decreased gym performance
  - Reduced strength
  - Decreased muscle pump
  - Heart rate variability changes
  - Training motivation drop
  - Mood changes
  - Appetite suppression
  - GI disruption
  - Sleep disruption
  - Illness/injuries
  - Loss of libido/menses
  
- **Overreaching vs Overtraining:**
  - Overreaching: Temporary decline, days to reverse
  - Overtraining: Chronic decline, weeks to months to reverse
  
- **Recovery Strategies:**
  - During workout: Rest times, intra-workout nutrition, volume autoregulation
  - Between workouts: Nutrition timing, sleep, relaxation
  - Between weeks: Light sessions, rest days, deloads

## Methodology Principles

### Body Composition Assessment Priority
From PNC2: "For overweight clients, their weight and waist measurements matter just as much, if not more, than their skinfolds."

### Caloric Minimums
From PNC2: "We generally don't recommend that our clients consume less than 1500 calories per day."

### Hydration Formula
From PNC2: "The recommended daily water intake is 1mL per calorie consumed."
- Pre-exercise: 500-600mL 2-3 hours before
- During exercise: 200-300mL every 10-20 minutes

### Metabolic Adaptation Warning
From PNC2: Poor sleep, chronic stress, and inadequate calories all contribute to metabolic slowdown and adaptation plateaus.

### Gut Health Connection
From PNC2: Gut dysbiosis linked to obesity, inflammation, insulin resistance, IBS, allergies, asthma, cardiovascular disease, and mental health issues.

### Menstrual Cycle Awareness
From review_process: Different phases affect performance, fluid retention, fatigue, and energy levels. Not applicable for all clients but critical when relevant.
